Current issue

Eight articles in this issue, spanning autonomous flight, propulsion prognostics, air traffic AI, and human factors in cockpit automation.

Research Article pp. 98–119

Deep Reinforcement Learning for Autonomous Taxiing in Congested Airport Surface Traffic

D. Kapoor, M. Reyes, S. Lindqvist

Trains a reinforcement learning agent on surface movement radar data to plan taxi routes that reduce runway incursion risk, validated across simulated traffic at three major hub airports.

Kapoor, D., Reyes, M., & Lindqvist, S. (2026). JATAIR, 5(2), 98–119. https://doi.org/10.5281/jatair.2026.0502.098

Survey pp. 74–97

Machine Learning for Predictive Maintenance in Turbofan Engines: A Systematic Review

A. Okonkwo, R. Bianchi

Surveys 52 studies applying machine learning to turbofan sensor data for remaining-useful-life prediction, identifying degradation labeling and sensor drift as the dominant open challenges.

Okonkwo, A., & Bianchi, R. (2026). JATAIR, 5(2), 74–97. https://doi.org/10.5281/jatair.2026.0502.074
View PDF
Research Article pp. 50–73

Vision-Based Sense-and-Avoid for Fixed-Wing UAVs Operating in Uncontrolled Airspace

T. Nakamura, F. Oduya, C. Becker

Presents a monocular vision pipeline for detecting and avoiding non-cooperative aircraft, achieving reliable detection at ranges sufficient for regulatory-compliant avoidance maneuvers.

Nakamura, T., Oduya, F., & Becker, C. (2026). JATAIR, 5(2), 50–73. https://doi.org/10.5281/jatair.2026.0502.050

Short Communication pp. 38–49

Pilot Trust Calibration Under Partial Autopilot Failures: A Simulator Study

C. Becker, H. Whitcombe

Reports a 48-pilot simulator study on how partial autopilot failure disclosures affect trust recovery and manual takeover timing during simulated approach phases.
